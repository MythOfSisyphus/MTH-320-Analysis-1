**Exercise 1.2.7.** Given a function $f$ and a subset $A$ of its domain, let $f(A)$ represent the range of $f$ over the set $A;$ that is, $f(A) = \{f(x) : x \in A\}$.

$(a)$ Let $f(x) = x^2$. If $A = [0, 2]$ and $B = [1, 4]$, find $f(A)$ and $f(B)$. Does $f(A \cap B) = f(A) \cap f(B)$ in this case? Does $f(A \cup B) = f(A) \cup f(B)$?

$(b)$ Find two sets $A$ and $B$ for which $f(A \cap B) \ne f(A) \cap f(B)$.

$(c)$ Show that, for an arbitrary function $g: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$, it is always true the $g(A \cap B) \subseteq g(A) \cap g(B)$ for all sets $A, B \subseteq \mathbb{R}$.

$(d)$ Form and prove a conjecture about the relationship between $g(A \cup B)$ and $g(A) \cup g(B)$ for an arbitrary function $g$.

**Solution:** $(a)$ $f(A) = [0, 4], f(B) = [1, 16], f(A \cap B) = [0, 1]$ and $f(A \cup B) = [0, 16]$.
Here $f(A) \cap f(B) = [0, 1]$ and $f(A) \cup f(B) = [0, 16]$, therefore $f(A \cap B) = f(A) \cap f(B)$ and $f(A \cup B) = f(A) \cup f(B)$ are both true.

$(b)$ Take $A = (-1, 0)$ and $B = (0, 1)$. We see that $f(A \cap B) = \emptyset$ whereas $f(A) \cap f(B) = (0, 1) \cap (0, 1) = (0, 1) \ne \emptyset$.

**Proof:** $(c)$ Let $g : \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ be arbitrary and let $A, B \subseteq \mathbb{R}$.
Take any $y \in g (A \cap B)$. By definition of image, there exists some $x \in A \cap B$ with $g(x) = y$. Since $x \in A$ and $x \in B$, the same $y$ is in both $g(A)$ and $g(B)$. Hence $y \in g(A) \cap g(B)$.

Because this holds for every $y \in g(A \cap B)$, we have
$$g(A \cap B) \subseteq g(A) \cap g(B).$$

$(d)$ **Conjecture:** $g(A \cup B) = g(A) \cup g(B)$.
**Proof:** For any
$$\begin{align*}
y \in g(A \cup B)
&\iff \exists x\,(x\in A\cup B \wedge g(x)=y) \\
&\iff \exists x\,((x \in A \vee  x \in B) \wedge g(x) = y) \\
&\iff \exists (x\, (x \in A \wedge g(x) = y)) \vee (x\, (x \in B \wedge g(x) = y)) \\
&\iff y \in g(A) \vee y \in g(B) \\
&\iff y \in g(A) \cup g(B)
\end{align*}$$
Therefore, $g(A \cup B) = g(A) \cup g(B)$.
